Methodology & Data Sources

This analysis focuses on three distinct skills that combine to define the ultimate and-1 artist:

1. Volume: Drawing Fouls

Measured by fouls drawn per game (FDPG) and career free throw attempts (FTA). A high FTA total means a player consistently put themselves in position to draw shooting fouls. Career FTA is the best proxy for pre-2005 players where play-by-play data doesn't exist.

2. Conversion Rate: Making the Shot While Fouled

Measured as and-1s made / total shooting fouls drawn. From 82games.com for the 2004-05 and 2005-06 seasons — the earliest available play-by-play based and-1 tracking data. The league average in 2005-06 was 28.2% of shooting fouls resulted in a completed basket. Players above 40% are elite finishers through contact.

3. Bonus Free Throw: Completing the Three-Point Play

Measured as bonus FT made / bonus FT attempted specifically on and-1 situations. Research confirms a player's bonus FT% closely mirrors their career FT% — there is no significant "clutch effect" on the bonus free throw. The best bonus FT shooters are simply the best free throw shooters overall.

The Great Paradox: Shaquille O'Neal

Shaq had the highest and-1 rate per shot attempt in the league in 2005-06 (8.5% of all his shot attempts ended in a made basket + foul drawn) and a 54.9% and-1 rate (% of shooting fouls that became and-1s). Yet he converted the bonus free throw at only 47.9% — far below the 72.7% league average. Defenders famously fouled him intentionally before he could release (Hack-a-Shaq) precisely because they feared the and-1.

Data Limitations

Play-by-play and-1 tracking only exists from ~2004-05 onward. No public database publishes a clean career and-1 total leaderboard. The three-point shooting fouls drawn data (Harden: 521 career) covers from approximately 2013-14 onward per NBA tracking. Pre-2005 era legends (Malone, Jordan, Chamberlain) are ranked on FTA volume only.
